# Competitive Landscape

> Last updated: March 9, 2026
> Review quarterly or when a competitor makes a major move

---

## Market Context

- **48.1 million Americans** played golf in 2025 — 8 consecutive years of growth, 50% increase over the past decade
- **83% of regular golfers** enjoy on-course betting games
- **75%+ of core golfers** (8+ rounds/year) have at least one golf app
- **41-44% of golf app users pay** for premium features (Hole19 data) — dramatically above the 2-5% industry average for apps
- Golf attracts a high-income demographic willing to pay for tools
- Female golfers up 58% since 2019 — market is diversifying

**Key insight:** Nobody has won the golf betting app market yet. It's fragmented, with most apps having mediocre UX and shallow game implementations.

### Golf GameBook — Primary Competitor (Watch Closely)

| | Details |
|--|---------|
| **Price** | Freemium. Free tier with GPS + basic scoring. Gold Membership unlocks premium features (price varies by region). |
| **Games** | 20 formats (Skins, Match Play, Scramble, Better Ball, team formats, side contests like Closest to Pin) |
| **Platforms** | iOS and Android |
| **Users** | 1.5 million registered users across 100+ countries. 50 million rounds recorded. 500K users in Scandinavia alone. |
| **Headquarters** | Espoo, Finland. Founded by PGA professionals. ~35-60 employees across 3 continents. |
| **Funding** | €3M round (investors include Carl Manneh/Minecraft founder, Fredrik Österberg/Evolution co-founder, Wolt founding investor). €2.5M round in September 2024 (Spanish and Nordic investors, including Álvaro Morata). Total raised: ~€5-6M. |
| **Key Partnerships** | **IMG worldwide partnership** (50+ tournaments/yr, 150+ managed courses). Golfmanager (Spain — embedded in course management systems). |
| **Strengths** | Best-funded company in the space. IMG distribution deal is the most powerful partnership in competitive set. Tournament features up to 72 players with Ryder Cup-style team competitions. "Featured Stars" influencer program launched Jan 2026 (Garrett Clark/GM Golf, Kai Trump, Morata). Social/community features. GPS on 42K courses. Lifestyle merch store. |
| **Weaknesses** | Positioning is "digital clubhouse" — social platform first, betting/games second. No educational content or SEO strategy (zero game guides, no blog with depth). Game implementations appear breadth-over-depth (20 formats but unclear on configurability, press support, edge cases). Not a "golf betting app" in their own messaging. Finnish origin = weaker brand recognition in US market. |
| **Model** | Social-first: live leaderboards, friend connections, following influencers. Monetizes through Gold Membership + merch. Distribution through IMG courses and influencer reach. |

**Why they matter:**
- The IMG partnership gives them access to 150+ premium courses worldwide — far more distribution than Skins App's Troon deal
- The influencer strategy (Garrett Clark alone has millions of YouTube followers) is a growth channel nobody else in the space is using
- Their tournament feature (72 players, multi-round, team competitions) directly overlaps with Stick's Events roadmap
- They're the only competitor with real venture funding (~€5-6M) and institutional investors
- They're growing internationally while every other competitor is US-only

**Where Stick still wins:**
- Game engine depth: press-the-press chains, hammer mode, zero-sum verification, multi-game scorecards — they appear to have breadth but not this level of configurability
- Content authority: they have zero SEO content. Stick already has 18 pages of deep educational content. They're competing on distribution; we're competing on authority.
- Betting-first identity: they say "digital clubhouse," we say "golf betting app." When someone searches "best golf betting app," we show up. They don't.
- US market focus: their strength is Scandinavia/Europe. Stick is built for American golf culture (Nassau, presses, Saturday morning foursomes)

**Threat assessment:** Not a direct threat today in the US golf betting search space. But the most likely company to become a serious threat if they (a) pivot harder into betting/games messaging, (b) leverage IMG to push into US courses, or (c) build out game depth. Monitor quarterly. Their influencer moves and tournament features are the things to watch most closely.

## Where Stick Wins

1. **Accuracy** — 1,017 tests, zero-sum verification. When money's on the line, correctness matters.
2. **Multi-game rounds** — One score entry feeds all games. Nobody else does this cleanly.
3. **Press depth** — Auto-press, press-the-press chains, configurable limits. Competitors get this wrong or skip it.
4. **Real-time sync** — Convex reactive backend, <2s updates to all viewers.
5. **Design quality** — Built by a designer, not just engineers.
6. **Viewer experience** — Non-scorekeepers are first-class citizens, not afterthoughts.
7. **Generous free tier** — 3 full rounds + capable free tier, not a 2-round gate or instant paywall.

## Where Competitors Win (For Now)

1. **Golf GameBook** — IMG partnership (150+ courses), €5-6M funding, 1.5M users, influencer strategy (Garrett Clark), tournament features up to 72 players. Most well-resourced competitor.
2. **Beezer** — 30 games vs our 12. Quantity, even if not quality.
3. **18 Birdies** — GPS + analytics + betting in one app. Massive existing user base.
4. **GolfApp** — Currently free. "Mayhem" prop bets are creative.
5. **Loop Golf** — Actual money settlement (we don't have this yet).
6. **Golf Bettor** — 60-day free trial is very generous.
7. ~~**Skins App** — Troon partnership gives distribution at 900+ properties.~~ App broken as of March 2026. Threat level downgraded.
