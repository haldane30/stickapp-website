# Content Opportunities Research for Stick Golf Betting App
## March 5, 2026

This document maps underserved content opportunities for stickapp.golf that serve the "group organizer" — the person who picks games, sets stakes, and settles bets. These are adjacent topics that catch the same audience but go beyond game rules.

---

## 1. GOLF TRIP PLANNING CONTENT

### Current Landscape

**What's Out There:**
- Heavy consolidation around travel logistics companies (Golfbreaks, TripCaddie, PerryGolf)
- Most content focuses on "where to go" and "how to book" (destination guides, accommodations, tee time coordination)
- Golf trip planning largely treated as vacation logistics, not as strategic experiences

**Existing Content Gaps:**
- No authority site owns "how to organize betting for a golf trip"
- Travel guides don't address the betting/stakes element at all
- No content on "games for a trip week" or "long-form Nassau strategy"
- TripCaddie has "Golf Trip Manager" tool but minimal educational content

**Search Volume/Competition:**
- "Golf trip planning" = moderate search volume, low competition (mostly travel agencies)
- "Golf buddy trip" = moderate volume, low quality
- "Guys golf trip planning" = moderate volume, generic destination coverage
- "Scottsdale golf trip planning" = geo-specific, heavily travel-focused

### Content Opportunity: **"How to Organize Golf Betting for a Group Trip"**

**Target Angles:**
1. **Multi-round betting across a week** — How to track Nassau/Skins over 3-4 rounds without resetting
2. **Trip-length side games** — Games that play well when you're together for multiple days (low-pace games that repeat well)
3. **Settling expenses with betting** — If the week-long Nassau compounds, how do guys actually settle it?
4. **Trip traditions and formats** — Why some groups always play the same game every day vs. rotating
5. **Handicap verification for trip play** — Preventing sandbagging on a trip (new players to the group)
6. **Phone/app-based scorekeeping for multiple days** — How to not lose track of running totals

**Stick Advantage:**
- Trip context is multi-round, real-time sync
- Stick's Settings and configurations let you create repeatable trip formats
- Settlement becomes clear when you're running the same game for 3 days

**Estimated Reach:**
- ~100k golfers per year book golf trips (major companies see 20-30k customers)
- Of those, ~40-50% play for money
- Unserved audience looking for "trip betting strategy" = maybe 10-15k yearly searches

**Viability: HIGH** — Unserved niche with high purchase intent (people planning trips are actively looking for value-add content)

## 2. TOURNAMENT/OUTING ORGANIZATION CONTENT

### Current Landscape

**What's Out There:**
- Heavy charity/corporate focus (tournament fundraising guides, event planning)
- Content clusters around "scramble format," "best-ball format," "closest to pin" side games
- Charity guides cover logistics but assume PGA-pro-level tournament infrastructure
- Company outing guides focus on team-building, not serious money games

**Existing Content Gaps:**
- No content on "friendly 12-16 player formats with betting"
- "Side games that work for scrambles" is a tag but no deep exploration
- Handicap adjustment for mixed-ability outings (10-handicap vs 18-handicap in same scramble) is not addressed
- No guide on "how much to stake for a charity outing" or "settling bets across multiple teams"
- No content connecting tournaments to apps (implied: you settle by hand, not digitally)

**Search Volume/Competition:**
- "Charity golf tournament format" = high volume, high quality (competitive space)
- "Company golf outing games" = moderate volume, thin content
- "Golf scramble betting" = moderate volume, scattered coverage
- "Golf tournament side games" = moderate volume, mostly listicles

### Content Opportunity: **"Side Games for Golf Outings & Tournaments"** (2-3 post series)

**Target Angles:**
1. **"Best Nassau Strategies for Outings"** — Nassau played in a 12-person outing (how does it work, settlement example)
2. **"Side Games That Work with Scrambles"** — Skins-style play alongside scramble scoring
3. **"How to Run Multiple Nassau Matches in a Tournament"** — If you have 3 teams of 4, how do people bet?
4. **"Settling Bets on the 19th Hole"** — The mechanics of who owes what after an outing
5. **"Handicap Adjustments for Mixed-Ability Outings"** — Setting fair games when skill spread is 5-20 handicap

**Stick Advantage:**
- Outings are where Events feature shines (multi-group leaderboard)
- Real-time scoring removes "someone made a math error" at the 19th hole
- Settlement becomes transparent (app shows who won what)

**Estimated Reach:**
- ~50k charity/corporate golf outings per year in US
- Of those, ~70% probably do some form of betting/side games
- Organizers searching for "how to run this fairly" = maybe 20-30k yearly searches

**Viability: MEDIUM-HIGH** — Growing outing market, low-quality existing content, Events feature makes Stick relevant here

## 3. GOLF HANDICAP AND SCORING QUESTIONS

### Current Landscape

**What's Out There:**
- USGA/NCGA own handicap index rules (authoritative but dense)
- Articles on "how to calculate net score" are abundant but technical
- Some content on "handicap in betting" but mostly focused on professional/betting app odds, not casual play
- No comprehensive guide on "which % of handicap for Nassau" or "how to apply handicap in Skins"

**Existing Content Gaps:**
- "What percentage of handicap do I use in Nassau?" — This is a FAQ with 5-6 common answers (80%, 90%, full HCP), no source of truth
- Net score calculation explained but not in a betting context (stroke-by-stroke walkthrough)
- Handicap tables (1-18 stroke holes) are explained in rules PDFs, not in betting guides
- "Gross vs. net scoring in different games" — Nassau uses net, Skins can use either, Quota is gross
- "How to handle handicap adjustments when someone doesn't have an official HCP"

**Search Volume/Competition:**
- "How do golf handicaps work in betting" = moderate volume, some good sources
- "What percentage of handicap for Nassau" = low volume but high intent (people actively playing)
- "How to calculate golf net scores betting" = moderate volume, educational content exists
- "Golf handicap in match play" = moderate volume, rules-focused

### Content Opportunity: **"Handicap Guide for Casual Golf Betting"** (comprehensive guide + quick reference)

**Target Angles:**
1. **"Handicap 101 for Betting"** — The minimum you need to know to use handicaps fairly
2. **"Nassau Handicap Rules: What % Do You Use?"** — Compare 80%, 90%, full handicap with examples
3. **"Handicap Tables for Golf Betting"** — Which holes get your strokes (the numbered scorecard holes)
4. **"Net Score Settlement Examples"** — Step-by-step Nassau example showing net calc
5. **"Adjusting Handicaps for Trip/Casual Play"** — When someone doesn't have a USGA HCP, what do you do?
6. **"Handicap Differences in Your Group"** — A 5-handicapper vs 18-handicapper: how to keep it fair
7. **Quick reference cheat sheet** — One-page printable for the first tee

**Stick Advantage:**
- Stick's "Handicap" reference guide already exists but could link to deeper blog content
- App auto-applies handicaps correctly (no ambiguity), so blog content teaches "why Stick does this"
- Settlement math becomes self-evident when you see Stick's calculations

**Estimated Reach:**
- ~500k casual golfers bet money games per year
- ~20% probably have questions about handicap application = 100k
- Searches = 50-100k yearly for variations of this topic

**Viability: HIGH** — Educational content, SEO-friendly, feeds app usage (people understand handicaps → use them in Stick)

**Viability: HIGH** — Cultural, evergreen content; builds brand authority beyond product; very on-brand for premium app

- "Golf opening day" = low volume, mostly sports news

**Estimated Reach:**
- Each season gets 20-50k searches for variations of seasonal golf betting

## 7. THE "HOW TO PLAY" ANGLE

### Current Landscape

**What's Out There:**
- Golf Digest, LiveAbout, Beezer, 18Birdies all have "how to play Nassau," "how to play Wolf," etc.
- These are brief tutorials (500-800 words), often in listicle format
- No deep walkthrough with multi-round examples
- No content on "common mistakes" or "strategy tips for beginners"

**Existing Content Gaps:**
Comparing Stick's existing game guides to competitor content:
- **Stick game guides:** 1500-2500 words, settlement examples, FAQ, internal linking, reference guides
- **Competitor content:** 300-800 words, basic rules, light strategy
- **Gap:** Nobody owns "how to actually master [game]" with depth

**What's Missing:**
1. **Multi-hole walkthroughs** — Showing a full 18-hole game with real scenarios
2. **Variant explanations** — What's a "press Nassau" vs. "standard Nassau"?
3. **Common beginner mistakes** — What new players get wrong
4. **Settlement math walkthroughs** — Who actually owes what at the end?
5. **Strategy tips specific to skill mix** — How to adjust tactics if you're the worst player
6. **Pressure-situation play** — What to do when you're down and facing the press

**Search Volume/Competition:**
- "How to play Nassau golf" = moderate-high volume, multiple decent sources
- "How to play Wolf golf game" = moderate-high volume, good Golf Digest coverage
- "How to keep score in Skins golf" = moderate volume, some good sources
- "How to play [specific game]" variations = 50k+ yearly searches

### Content Opportunity: **Deep-Dive "How to Play" Blog Posts + Video Shorts**

**Strategy:**
Stick's existing game guides live at `/games/[slug]/` and are comprehensive. But blog content could explore a different angle:

**Target Angles:**
1. **Beginner vs. Advanced Versions** — "Nassau for Beginners" vs. "Nassau With Presses and Carryovers"
2. **Variant Guides** — "Chicago 39 Quota," "Umbrella Scotch," "Hammer Wolf"

6. **Skill-Adjusted Play** — "How to Sandbag-Proof Your Game"

**Stick Advantage:**

**Estimated Reach:**
- "How to play" queries = 100-150k yearly searches
- Beginner queries likely dominant (50-60% of volume)

**Viability: MEDIUM-HIGH** — Existing content (guides) exists but could feed blog/video ecosystem

## 8. COMPETITOR CONTENT ANALYSIS

### Key Findings:

**Beezer Golf** (Primary competitor)
- Has 300-500 word guides for each game
- Strong SEO presence (ranks well for "how to play [game]")
- Settlement examples are light and often incorrect (flagged in prior audits)
- No reference guides or authority content
- No trip/outing content
- No etiquette content

**18Birdies**
- Ranks for "tips to play" variations
- Knowledge base format, not blog
- No comparative content
- No settlement math, mostly visual rules

**Skins App**
- Light educational content
- Mostly focuses on app features, not teaching games
- No blog/SEO strategy

**GolfApp.com**
- Almost no content marketing
- Very thin game guides

### Strategic Implication:
Stick is positioned to **own the content authority** in golf betting. Competitors are not competing on educational content—they're relying on app UX. This is a MAJOR opportunity.

## FINAL NOTES

The group organizer persona is starved for content that goes beyond "here's what a Nassau is." They want:
- **Practical guidance** on managing bets across time/people
- **Cultural authority** on how to bet responsibly with friends
- **Confidence** that they're playing fairly and settling correctly
- **Frameworks** for choosing which game to play

Stick is uniquely positioned to own this space because the app itself solves the hardest part (settlement calculation), so blog content can focus on the strategic/cultural layer rather than the mechanics.

The CNAME (stickapp.golf) + 12 games + reference guides already position Stick as the authority. Content gaps are mostly in "adjacent" topics, not core game knowledge.
