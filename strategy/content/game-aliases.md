## Nassau

**Primary name:** Nassau

**Alternate names:**
- 2-2-2 or 3-3-3 (refers to the dollar amounts, e.g., "$2 Nassau" = $2 front, $2 back, $2 overall)
- Best Nines (rare)

**Related terms people search:**
- Nassau bet, Nassau golf bet, Nassau wager
- Golf press bet (press is a Nassau mechanic)
- Front nine back nine bet
- How to play Nassau

**Component terms:**
- Press, auto-press, press the press
- Aloha (final hole side bet sometimes paired with Nassau)

**Notes:** Nassau is universally recognized. The "2-2-2" notation is conversational but not heavily searched as a standalone term. Press-related searches are a major long-tail opportunity and deserve their own blog posts.

## Snake

**Primary name:** Snake

**Alternate names:**
- Animals / Animal Game (broader category — Snake is one "animal")
- Zoo Golf (the collection of animal-themed penalties)
- Golfimals (rare, informal)

**Component "animals" (when played as the full Zoo game):**
- Snake = 3-putt
- Camel = sand trap (bunker)
- Frog = hitting into water
- Gorilla / Ape = hitting out of bounds
- Skunk = double bogey or worse

**Related terms people search:**
- Snake golf game, snake putting game, 3 putt game
- Animal golf game, zoo golf game
- Golf three putt penalty game

**Notes:** Many groups only play "Snake" (the 3-putt version) without the full zoo. Our guide should cover Snake as the primary game and mention the zoo/animals expansion as a variation. "3 putt game" is a strong keyword.

## Match Play

**Primary name:** Match Play

**Alternate names / related formats:**
- Foursomes (pairs, one ball, alternate shots)
- Alternate Shot (same as foursomes)
- Four-Ball (pairs, everyone plays own ball, best ball counts)
- Greensomes / Modified Alternate Shot / Scotch Foursomes (both tee off, pick best drive, alternate from there)
- Bloodsome (opponent picks which drive you play — punishing variant)

**Related terms people search:**
- Match play golf rules, match play scoring
- How does match play work in golf
- Match play vs stroke play
- Match play press, match play with presses

**Notes:** Match play is a broad format used across many contexts (Ryder Cup, club championships, casual betting). Our guide should focus on match play as a betting game between friends, not tournament match play. Distinguish from the foursomes/four-ball team formats.

## Nine Point

**Alternate names:**

**Related terms people search:**
- Nine point golf game, nines golf game
- 5-3-1 golf game, 5 3 1 golf
- Three player golf games (Nine Point is a top result for this)
- Golf game for 3 players

**Notes:** "Nines" and "Nine Point" are both common. Use both in the opening paragraph. The "best golf games for 3 players" blog post should link heavily to this guide. No app currently ranks for this keyword — easy win.

## Split Sixes

**Primary name:** Split Sixes

**Alternate names:**
- English (common alternate, especially in certain US regions)
- Cricket (less common, can confuse with the bar game)
- 6-Point Game (sometimes — but also used for Scotch, so ambiguous)
- Triples (rare, sometimes synonymous)
- 4-2-0 Game (describes the point distribution — occasionally used)

**Related terms people search:**
- Split sixes golf, split sixes rules, how to play split sixes
- English golf game, English golf betting game
- 6 point golf game (ambiguous — could be Split Sixes or Scotch)
- Three player golf betting games, best golf games for 3 players
- 4 2 0 golf game, four two zero golf
- Golf game where you split points, point splitting golf game

**FAQ candidates:**
- "What's the difference between Split Sixes and Sixes?" (different games — 3-player individual points vs. 4-player rotating partnerships)
- "What's the difference between Split Sixes and Nines?" (6 points per hole with 4-2-0 vs. 9 points per hole with 5-3-1; Split Sixes is more punitive — high scorer gets 0)
- "Is English the same as Split Sixes?" (yes)
- "What is a blitz in Split Sixes?" (beating both opponents by 2+ strokes = sweep all 6 points)
- "How do you handle ties in Split Sixes?" (two tied for best = 3-3-0, two tied for worst = 4-1-1, three-way tie = 2-2-2)

**Notes:** "English" is genuinely common in certain regions. Important to distinguish from Sixes/Hollywood (which is a 4-player game). The Split Sixes vs. Nines confusion is the strongest SEO angle — both are 3-player point games but use different distributions. The guide should make this comparison prominently and link to Nine Point. "Best golf games for 3 players" blog post should link heavily here.

## Sixes

**Primary name:** Sixes

**Alternate names:**
- Hollywood (very common — used interchangeably with Sixes)
- Round Robin (common, especially in organized/tournament play)
- Six-Six-Six / 6-6-6 (descriptive — three 6-hole segments. CAUTION: also refers to a different format where the game *type* changes every 6 holes, e.g., scramble → best-ball → alternate shot)

**Related terms people search:**
- Sixes golf game, Hollywood golf game, round robin golf
- Rotating partners golf game, golf game where teams change
- 6 6 6 golf format, six six six golf
- Best ball rotating partners, fourball round robin
- How to play Hollywood golf, Hollywood golf rules

**FAQ candidates:**
- "What's the difference between Sixes and Split Sixes?" (different games — 4-player teams vs. 3-player individual points)
- "Is Hollywood the same as Sixes?" (yes)
- "Is Round Robin the same as Sixes?" (yes)
- "What is 6-6-6 in golf?" (ambiguous — could be Sixes or a format-rotation game)

**Notes:** Three roughly equal search terms (Sixes, Hollywood, Round Robin). Use all three in the opening paragraph. "Six-Six-Six" needs careful handling because it sometimes refers to a scramble/alternate-shot/best-ball rotation format that changes every 6 holes — which is NOT the same game. Clarify this in the guide. Sixes requires exactly 4 players — distinguish from Split Sixes (3 players) throughout.

## Junk

**Primary name:** Junk

**Alternate names:**
- Dots (very common — the original name from marking dots on scorecards)
- Trash (very common)
- Garbage (common)
- The Dot Game (less common)
- Side Bets (generic description, not a name per se)

**Component side bets (each is its own searchable term):**
- Greenies (closest to pin on par 3, must make par)
- Sandies (par or better from a bunker)
- Barkies (par or better after hitting a tree)
- Arnies (par on a par 5 without hitting the green in regulation)
- Chippies (up and down from off the green)
- Poleys / Pole Winners (longest putt made)
- Ferrets (holed from off the green)

**Related terms people search:**
- Junk golf game, dots golf game, trash golf game, garbage golf game
- Golf side bets, golf side games, golf junk bets
- Greenies sandies barkies
- What are junk bets in golf, what are dots in golf

**Notes:** THIS IS THE HIGHEST-VALUE ALIAS OPPORTUNITY. People search for this game under four different names with roughly equal frequency. Stick calls it "Junk" — the guide should open with all four names prominently. The component side bets (greenies, sandies, barkies) are each their own keyword target. Consider a dedicated section or even a companion blog post covering individual dot types.

## Vegas

**Primary name:** Vegas

**Alternate names:**
- Las Vegas (full name, used interchangeably)
- Daytona (distinct variant — both-over-par = high score first)

**Related but separate games:**
- Amigos (rotating partners based on fairway position — different game, often confused with Vegas)
- Monte Carlo (multiply scores instead of pairing — technically a distinct variant)
- Newtown (two middle balls vs outer balls — niche variant)

**Related terms people search:**
- Vegas golf game, Vegas golf rules
- Las Vegas golf game, Las Vegas golf betting
- Vegas golf betting game, how to play Vegas golf
- Daytona golf game, Daytona golf rules
- Golf game with two digit scores, score pairing golf
- Flip the bird golf, birdie flip golf game
- Team golf betting games

**Notes:** Vegas and Las Vegas are used interchangeably. Daytona is a recognized variant worth covering in depth. Amigos is a separate game that gets mentioned alongside Vegas but has different mechanics (rotating partners, ladder scoring). Monte Carlo is niche but worth a brief mention. "Two digit scores" and "score pairing" are how people describe it when they can't remember the name — good long-tail targets.

## Scotch

**Primary name:** Scotch (or Six-Point Scotch)

**Alternate names:**
- Six-Point Scotch / 6-Point Scotch (full formal name)
- 6-Point / 6-Point Game (what many groups call it colloquially — AMBIGUOUS, see below)
- 5-Point Scotch (drops one category, usually Prox — Beezer supports this)
- 7-Point Scotch (adds a bonus category)
- Low Ball–Low Total (describes the two main categories, not a separate game)

**CRITICAL DISTINCTION:** Do NOT confuse with:
- Scotch Foursomes (alternate shot format — different game entirely)
- Greensomes / Modified Alternate Shot / Canadian Foursomes / Pinehurst (both tee off, pick best drive, alternate from there — also different)
- Split Sixes / 6-Point Game for 3 players (individual points game, 4-2-0 system — different game)

**Related terms people search:**
- Scotch golf game, six point scotch golf, scotch golf rules
- 6 point golf game (AMBIGUOUS — could be Scotch or Split Sixes)
- Scotch foursomes rules (different game — but our guide should clarify)
- Team golf game with points, low ball low total golf
- Golf umbrella rule, golf blitz rule, sweep points golf
- How to play scotch golf, scotch golf betting game
- Best golf games for foursomes, 4 player golf betting games

**FAQ candidates:**
- "Is Scotch the same as Scotch Foursomes?" (no — completely different games)
- "Is 6-Point the same as Scotch?" (usually yes, but could also mean Split Sixes — ask player count)
- "What is the umbrella rule in golf?" (win all points on a hole = points double)
- "What happens when scores tie in Scotch?" (varies — some groups split, some void)
- "Can you press in Scotch?" (uncommon but possible)

**Notes:** "Golf's most popular and confusing gambling game" per Chicago Golf Guy. The Scotch vs. Scotch Foursomes confusion is extremely common and a major FAQ/SEO opportunity. "6-Point" is the most ambiguous term — always clarify player count. Stick has a competitive advantage here: umbrella, roll/hammer, high ball, eagle bonus, natural birdie toggle — more depth than any competitor.

**Notes:** Your buddy calling it "6 point" without knowing it's Scotch is extremely common. The guide should open by acknowledging this: "If your group plays something called '6 point' — this is probably it." The Scotch vs. Scotch Foursomes confusion is a strong FAQ candidate.

## Quota

**Primary name:** Quota

**Alternate names:**
- Chicago (equally common, possibly more common in some regions)
- Point Quota
- Points Game (generic)
- Target Score (descriptive, not a formal name)
- Stableford (related but distinct — Stableford is the official format, Quota adds the handicap-based target)
- Modified Stableford (PGA Tour variant with different point scale — related but not the same game)

**Related terms people search:**
- Quota golf game, Chicago golf game, how to play quota golf
- Point quota golf, quota system golf, quota golf rules
- Golf game based on handicap, handicap-based golf game
- Golf tournament format for large groups, best golf game for large groups
- Stableford golf scoring, stableford points system
- Modified stableford golf, barracuda championship format
- Golf outing format, golf league format, weekly golf game format
- 36 minus handicap golf, target score golf

**FAQ candidates:**
- "Is Chicago the same as Quota?" (yes — same game, different regional names)
- "What's the difference between Quota and Stableford?" (Stableford is the point system; Quota adds a personal target based on handicap. Beat your target to win.)
- "What's the difference between Quota and Modified Stableford?" (Modified Stableford uses a different point scale with negatives for bogey and worse — it's what the PGA Tour uses at the Barracuda Championship)
- "How do you calculate your quota in golf?" (36 minus your course handicap. A 15-handicap has a quota of 21.)
- "Can you play Quota with a large group?" (yes — it works for any number of players because everyone plays their own ball and settles based on individual performance)
- "What is Chicago 39 in golf?" (a harder variant where the base is 39 instead of 36, meaning everyone needs 3 more points to beat quota)

**Notes:** "Chicago" and "Quota" are interchangeable — use both in the opening paragraph. The "large groups" angle is the strongest SEO play — nobody else is positioning a specific game as the answer to "best golf game for 20+ players." The Stableford/Modified Stableford distinction is a key FAQ because golfers confuse them constantly. The 36-base vs 39-base (Chicago variant) is a configurable toggle in Stick and worth explaining clearly. The gross-scoring model (handicap in the target, not per-hole) is counterintuitive to golfers used to net games and deserves emphasis.

## Games Stick Doesn't Track (But We Cover Educationally)

These are popular golf betting games that appear regularly in searches but are not in Stick's 12-game lineup. We should have content about them to maintain topical authority, with honest disclosure that Stick doesn't currently track them.

### Bingo Bango Bongo (BBB)
- **Search volume:** HIGH — Golf Digest, Golf.com, Left Rough, 18Birdies, Beezer all have dedicated content
- **How it works:** 3 points per hole — Bingo (first on green), Bango (closest to pin once all on green), Bongo (first to hole out)
- **Why it matters for us:** Beginner-friendly, favors new golfers, often recommended as an entry-level betting game. Our Junk guide covers similar ground.
- **Content plan:** OPP-3 in Q2-2026 discovery. Educational blog post ending with Junk comparison.
- **Key alias:** BBB

### Banker
- **Search volume:** MEDIUM — Golf Digest has a standalone piece ("The game that requires you to carry cash")
- **How it works:** One player per hole is the Banker. Others bet against the Banker. Banker role passes to whoever birdied last hole.
- **Why it matters for us:** Supported by Beezer, 18Birdies, Skins App. Comes up in "best golf betting games" roundups.
- **Content plan:** OPP-5 in Q2-2026 discovery. Educational blog post.

### Aces and Deuces (Acey Ducey)
- **Search volume:** LOW-MEDIUM — Beezer has dedicated page. GolfCompendium, GolfWeGo have coverage.
- **How it works:** Low score on each hole (Ace) wins from all three others. High score (Deuce) pays all three others.
- **Why it matters for us:** Beezer differentiator. Appears in "all golf betting games" lists.
- **Content plan:** OPP-6 in Q2-2026 discovery. Short educational blog post.

### Amigos
- **Search volume:** LOW — GolfWRX thread, Beezer page
- **How it works:** Rotating partner format that scales to any group size (5, 6, 7, 8+). Players pay up the finishing order each hole.
- **Why it matters for us:** Strong angle for "golf games for 5+ players" content.
- **Content plan:** Mention in T3-4 (Odd Numbers post).

## Newly Discovered Aliases (Q2 2026 Update)

### Nassau additions
- **Aloha bet:** A one-hole side bet sometimes paired with Nassau on the final hole. People search "what is an Aloha bet in golf."

### Skins additions
- **Validated Skins:** A variant where you must match or beat your score on the following hole to "validate" the skin you just won. Prevents one hot hole from winning everything.
- **Stooges:** Very rare regional slang for Skins. Low search value, worth documenting.

### Wolf additions
- **Boss Man:** Occasionally used for the Wolf role itself ("who's Boss Man this hole?"). Very low frequency.
- **Wolfpack:** Used loosely when playing Wolf with 5+ players.

### Junk/Dots additions
- **Poleys:** Alternate spelling of "Polies" for the longest-putt side bet category. Both spellings appear in forums.
- **Closest in Regulation:** How some groups describe a Greenie-style bet on par 3s without using the term "greenie."

### Vegas additions
- **Flip Vegas / Vegas with Flip:** When a team with a birdie can "flip" their two-digit score (34 becomes 43). Creates a scoring variant some groups call "Flip."

### Scotch additions
- **Umbriago:** A very niche variant of the Umbrella rule that appears in some Chicago-area club traditions. GolfCompendium has a dedicated page. Very low frequency.

### Quota/Chicago additions
- **Quota 36:** How players refer to the standard version (36 minus handicap = target).
- **Chicago 39 / Chicago Points:** The harder variant using 39 as the base. Stick supports this as a toggle. Both terms search separately.
