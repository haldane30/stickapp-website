# Stick — Strategic Insights

Running list of non-obvious observations that surfaced during research and sessions. These inform strategy but don't need to be in active context every session.

## Content & SEO Observations

**Prox in Junk heavily favors low handicappers** (March 5, 2026) — Proximity in regulation rewards ball-striking ability, which correlates with low handicaps. High-handicap players almost never win prox bets. Worth noting in any high-handicapper blog post and potentially a future app feature (handicap-adjusted prox, or optional removal from Junk categories).

**"Fun golf games" is an untapped entry point** (March 5, 2026) — Golfers searching "fun golf games with friends" don't think of themselves as bettors, but they'd absolutely play Wolf or Skins if someone explained it. Massive top-of-funnel opportunity nobody in the betting app space is capturing. Pipeline T1B-1 targets this.

**Dispute resolution is a content goldmine** (March 5, 2026) — Golfers standing in parking lots Googling "do skins carry over on the last hole" are the exact person who needs Stick. A category of short, definitive answer posts (T2B series) could capture these micro-moments. Zero competition.

**The group organizer is the customer** (March 5, 2026) — The person who texts the group chat, picks the game, explains the rules, and settles the bets is the person who pays $59.99/year. Content that speaks directly to this persona (T3B series) has zero competition and high conversion potential.

**Competitors are asleep** (March 5, 2026) — Beezer's last blog post is from 2020. Skins App has ~6 content pages. 18 Birdies treats betting as an afterthought. Nobody has a content strategy. Stick's 20 pages already exceeds every competitor's content library combined.

## Gemini AI Framework Review — Key Suggestions (March 29, 2026)

Fed our Content Framework to Gemini for a second opinion. Assessment: "top 1% of content strategies." Actionable ideas surfaced:

1. **Interactive web calculators (HIGH PRIORITY, post-launch)** — Expose lightweight versions of Stick's settlement calculators on the website (e.g., `stickapp.golf/tools/nassau-calculator`). Interactive tools attract organic backlinks better than written content. Already identified as Strategy A in Session 11. Build once app engines are stable.

2. **Proprietary data journalism (MEDIUM PRIORITY, needs 1,000+ rounds)** — Once the app has meaningful usage data, publish insights like "Based on 10,000 rounds logged in Stick, 62% of Nassau matches see a press on hole 14." This is proprietary data nobody else can write — the ultimate E-E-A-T and AI citation signal. Target Q4 2026 or later.

3. **Visual content — diagrams, scorecards, flowcharts (MEDIUM PRIORITY)** — Our guides are entirely text-based. Clean visual diagrams of press chains, carryover settlements, or Wolf rotation order would be cited by AI systems and shared by golfers. Start with 2-3 key diagrams for highest-traffic guides (Nassau press chain, Skins carryover, Wolf rotation).

4. **Alias-forward title tags (LOW EFFORT, do during retroactive audit)** — Title tags should carry top aliases more aggressively. Example: "Junk Golf Rules (Also Called Dots, Trash & Garbage) | Stick Golf". Check and fix during retroactive audit pass.

5. **App Store review schema (AFTER LAUNCH, needs reviews)** — Once we have App Store ratings, the SoftwareApplication schema should pull in star ratings. A 4.9-star rating in Google search results for "best golf betting app" is a massive CTR boost.

6. **Traditional SEO patience (MINDSET)** — Traditional Google SEO on a new domain is a 12-18 month game. Early wins will come from AI search (Perplexity, ChatGPT), not Google blue links. Don't abandon the strategy at month 5 if rankings feel slow.
