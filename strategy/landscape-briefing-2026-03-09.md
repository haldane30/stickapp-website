# Landscape Briefing — March 9, 2026

## TL;DR

Competitors remain asleep. Beezer hasn't published new blog content, Skins App is coasting on their Troon partnership from mid-2024 with no visible content moves, and no major golf tech company has added betting features. The biggest development in the golf betting space is the PGA TOUR LIVE Betcast with DraftKings/ESPN — but that's spectator betting, not on-course games, so it's a different market entirely. With The Players Championship this week (March 12-15) and Masters exactly one month out (April 9-12), this is peak content season for Stick.

## Competitor Watch

**Beezer Golf** — No new blog posts detected. Their blog content still appears to date from 2020-era posts. They continue to claim "28+ side games" and "300+ golf games" in marketing copy, but their content library hasn't grown. Their games guide page exists but remains thin. Still the strongest SEO competitor by default, but they're not investing in content. **Moat continues to widen.**

**Skins App** — Riding the Troon partnership announced June 2024. No new content, no visible product updates in search. They list Wolf, Banker, Vegas, Hammer, and Skins as supported games. Their website (skinsapp.com) is marketing-focused with no educational content. The Troon deal gives them distribution at Troon-managed courses, but they're not converting that into search presence or content authority. **Still the biggest distribution threat, but not a content threat.**

**18 Birdies** — Unchanged. Still GPS-first with betting as a secondary feature. They offer ~10 games (Sixes, Skins, Wolf, Match Play, Points, Nassau, Vegas, Dots, Rolling Stroke, Targets). Their content strategy is a knowledge base / help center approach, not SEO-optimized guides. No signs of expanding their betting feature set. **Not a threat in content or depth.**

**GolfApp.com** — Still free, still casual. Supports Nassau, Skins, Match Play, Stroke Play, Nine Point, Closeout, Wolf, and "7 Point." They mention "tournament and league support coming soon" — worth monitoring but no evidence of actual launch. **Watch list, low threat.**

**Golf Bettor** — Quietly solid. 12 game formats with 65 different options, 18,000 courses. $9.99/year (cheapest in market). Recent updates were minor bug fixes (back nine bet config loading, keyboard dismiss button). User reviews are positive — described as "best app for managing golf games" with a responsive developer. **Worth monitoring. The $9.99 price point and 65 game options are competitive talking points.**

## New Entrants / Threats

**GolfSnap** — This one wasn't in the original competitive landscape doc. It's a golf betting & scorecard app at $39.99/year (or $3/month). Supports 20+ games including Alternate Shot, Bank, Better Ball, Bingo Bango Bongo, Closeout, Dots/Junk, Las Vegas, Match Play, Modified Stableford, Nassau, Nine Point, Scotch, Skins, Wolf, and more. Has a 5.0 rating but only 17 ratings. Small but feature-rich. Their website (golfsnap.app) is basic. **Recommend adding to the competitive landscape doc. Not an immediate threat but worth tracking.**

**Golf GameBook** — Claims to be "#1 golf scorecard" with "20+ game formats" and live leaderboards. Social/community angle. Not new but wasn't in the original competitive analysis. **Worth a closer look.**

**PGA TOUR LIVE Betcast (DraftKings/ESPN)** — New for 2026. A "watch-and-bet" experience for spectator golf, covering 12 marquee PGA TOUR events with 400+ hours of live betting coverage. This is sportsbook betting (DraftKings), not on-course friend-group games. **Different market, but it's normalizing "golf + betting" as a category, which benefits Stick's search presence.**

**Major golf tech (Arccos, Garmin, Shot Scope)** — No betting features added. All remain focused on shot tracking and analytics. **All clear.**

Not directly relevant to on-course betting games, but golf attention = golf search volume = more eyes on "how to play skins" type queries.
