## Competitor Watch

**Beezer Golf** — No change. Blog last updated ~2020. Website still positions as "the perfect scorecard" with 20+ games and 38,000-course GPS. They remain the game-count leader but continue to do zero content marketing. Their Apple Watch sync issues keep surfacing in reviews. Still the biggest SEO competitor purely because their old content ranks, but they're not investing in it.

**Skins App** — No new content or feature announcements. The Troon partnership from mid-2024 is still their most recent press. Priced at $40/year. App Store reviews are generally positive but users complain about the handicap index input — it uses a dial pad without a decimal point, forcing you to round. That's the kind of UX friction Stick can highlight. Still no content strategy whatsoever.

**18 Birdies** — Holding steady at $99.99/year with ~10 games. Betting remains a secondary feature behind GPS. No new betting-related content or feature updates. They published a "Top 3 Side Games" article at some point but nothing fresh. Not a direct threat to Stick's positioning.

**GolfApp** — Free option with ~8 game types. Added a "Mayhem" feature for creative mid-round bets (challenges, dares, press bets). Tournament and league support listed as "coming soon." Worth monitoring — the free tier could attract casual players, but feature depth is thin.

**Golf Bettor** — Still $9.99/year. Recent updates are all bug fixes (back nine config loading, crashes, GHIN updates). Developer is responsive to reviews. Remains the budget option with a basic interface and no content presence.

**Summary:** Nobody moved. The competitive moat from content alone continues to widen. Stick's 20+ pages of SEO content exceed every competitor's content library combined, and that gap grows every week.

## New Entrants / Threats

**Fairgame Golf — NEW WATCH LIST ITEM**

Fairgame is positioning aggressively as "golf's first digital clubhouse." Key details:

- **Free tier** with Skins, Match Play, and Nines
- **Built-in Fairgame Handicap Index** (free with every account)
- **Payment integration planned** (ApplePay/Venmo settlement in-app — not yet live)
- **Golf.com coverage** — they got a Golf.com article about their "Fairgame Pro" launch, which is meaningful press that none of the other small competitors have gotten
- **Social angle** — positioning as a social platform for golfers, not just a betting tool

Fairgame is early-stage and still building features, but the "free + social + handicap" combination could attract casual users. Their payment integration roadmap is worth watching — if they ship in-app settlement before Stick launches, that's a talking point for their marketing.

**Threat level:** Low-medium. They're going after a broader "social golf" market rather than deep betting, but the overlap is real.

**GPS/Hardware Companies (Arccos, Garmin, Shot Scope):** No betting features added. These companies remain focused on shot tracking and analytics. All clear.
