## Template 1: Cold Outreach — Free Product (Phase 1)

**Use Case:** Reaching out to micro-influencers you've never contacted before, offering free 1-year Stick Pro access

**Where to send:** Email (personal outreach > DM for professionalism)

**Subject Line Options:**
- "Stick Golf — Free 1-year access for your followers"
- "Quick idea for your next golf content"
- "[Creator name], your Nassau content is exactly why we built Stick"
- "Golf betting app for foursomes — free for you to try"

**Email Body (Customize Below):**

```
Hi [Creator First Name],

I've been watching your [SPECIFIC CONTENT TYPE] for a while — your Nassau breakdown last month was spot-on.

Quick context: I'm Justin, founder of Stick. We just built an app that automates Nassau, Skins, Wolf, and 10 other betting games for foursomes. It handles all the settlement math instantly, so your group can actually focus on playing.

Your audience clearly loves golf games and the competitive side of it. Thought you might enjoy testing Stick with your crew.

I'd like to send you a free 1-year Stick Pro access. No obligation — if it works for your Saturday rounds and you want to share a clip with your followers, awesome. If not, enjoy the free year anyway.

Interested?

—Justin
Founder, Stick Golf
stickapp.golf

P.S. — Happy to answer any questions about the app or how it works.
```

**Customization Checklist:**
- [ ] Replace [SPECIFIC CONTENT TYPE] with actual video they made (e.g., "your Nassau vs Skins comparison", "your weekend vlog from Torrey Pines")
- [ ] Replace [Creator First Name] with their real first name
- [ ] Link stickapp.golf (or /download, /about, whatever page is most relevant)
- [ ] Keep total length under 150 words (aim for 120–140)
- [ ] Send Tues–Thurs, 10 AM–2 PM (best open rates)
- [ ] Follow up 1x after 7 days if no response (don't be pushy)

## Template 2: Cold Outreach — Specific Compliment (Higher Conversion)

**Use Case:** Same as Template 1, but with deeper personalization. Slightly longer but higher conversion rate.

**Subject Line:** "[Creator name], your [specific thing] inspired us to build Stick"

**Email Body:**

```
Hi [First Name],

Your video on [SPECIFIC MOMENT/CONTENT] — where [SPECIFIC DETAIL about their content] — is exactly why I built Stick.

Most golf betting apps are designed by people who don't actually play Nassau or Skins with their buddies. They treat it like fantasy football. Your content shows you get the real thing: the banter, the stakes, the moment someone presses and you realize the whole match just changed.

That's what Stick is built for. We automate the math so that moment stays fun instead of turning into a 10-minute settlement argument.

I'd love to get Stick in your hands and see if it makes your Saturday rounds better. Free 1-year access, no strings.

If you want to share a clip with your followers, that'd be amazing. If not, no pressure — just enjoy having one less thing to track.

Down to try?

—Justin
Founder, Stick Golf
stickapp.golf
```

**Why This Works:**
- Specific compliment signals you actually watched their content (not automated outreach)
- Emotional hook: "You understand the real thing" (people love being understood)
- Clear value prop: Solves the exact problem their audience has
- Low pressure: "If not, no pressure" reduces decision friction
- Personal signature: Shows you're real, not a bot

**Customization Checklist:**
- [ ] Replace [SPECIFIC MOMENT/CONTENT] with actual detail (e.g., "your round at Pebble where someone pressed after the eagle on #7")
- [ ] Replace [SPECIFIC DETAIL] with the actual detail (e.g., "everyone froze for a second before the reactions started")
- [ ] 150–200 words is fine for this version
- [ ] Send Tues–Thurs

- If they say no, don't push
- If they say yes, set up quickly (referral link + payment method)

## Finding Creators: A Search Strategy

### Where to Find Micro-Influencers

**Platform 1: Creators.co**
- Go to creators.co
- Search "golf" (category)
- Filter by platform (TikTok, Instagram, YouTube)
- Filter by follower count (10K–100K)
- Sort by engagement rate (highest first)
- Result: List of 50+ creators with email addresses

**Platform 2: Influencer.co**
- Similar to above
- Better for email finding
- Slightly more expensive but more accurate data

**Platform 3: HypeAuditor / Modash**
- YouTube-focused
- Heepsy is another good option
- All have free trials

**Platform 4: Manual Search**
- Go to TikTok
- Search: "#golftiktok #golfcontent #golfvlog"
- Look at videos with 5K–20K views (sweet spot for micro-influencers)
- Check creator's follower count
- If 10K–100K and they seem authentic, note them
- Try to find email from their bio (link-in-bio or website)

**Platform 5: Reddit & GolfWRX**
- Find active commenters in r/golf
- Check their post history
- If they have YouTube/Instagram, reach out
- Already warm intro ("I saw you on Reddit")

### How to Find Email Addresses

**Method 1: Bio Link**
- Creator's Instagram bio might link to website
- Website often has "contact" or email signup
- Email is usually visible or findable

**Method 2: Creator Databases**
- Creators.co, Influencer.co, Heepsy all have email
- Usually premium feature ($20–50/month buys access to many creators)

**Method 3: Hunter.io / RocketReach**
- Paste creator's name + Instagram handle
- Sometimes finds email
- Free tier limited

**Method 4: Direct DM**
- If you can't find email, DM on Instagram: "Hey — would love to collab. What's the best email to reach you?"
- More casual, lower response rate, but works sometimes

**Method 5: Google Search**
- "[Creator name] contact" or "[Creator name] business email"
- Sometimes surfaces interviews, podcast appearances with email

## Red Flags & What to Avoid

**Red Flag 1: Creator Asks for Payment Upfront Before Trial**
- Normal for mega-influencers (1M+)
- For micro-influencers, this is a sign they're overvaluing themselves
- Recommendation: Pass, move on

**Red Flag 2: Creator Demands Exclusivity**
- "I'll only work with Stick if you agree I can't work with other golf apps"
- Micro-influencers shouldn't make this demand
- Recommendation: Pass

**Red Flag 3: Creator Wants Script/Full Control**
- "I'll only post if you approve every word"
- Kills authenticity
- Recommendation: Clarify your non-negotiables (brand safety), then let them create

**Red Flag 4: Creator Gets Huge Engagement Spike Then Disappears**
- Posts once, gets 10K views, then ghost
- Sign they're not building a real audience, just chasing trends
- Recommendation: Build relationships with consistent creators, not flash-in-the-pan

**Red Flag 5: Creator's Followers Seem Fake**
- Comments are all emojis, generic spam
- Follower count is high but engagement is <1%
- Recommendation: Pass (their audience won't convert)

**Green Flag 1: Creator Responds to DM Within 24 Hours**
- Shows they're engaged, not just collecting followers
- Indication they'll be responsive partner

**Green Flag 2: Creator Asks Questions About the App**
- "How does the press system work?" or "What sports does it cover?"
- Shows genuine interest, not just taking free product
- Best collaborators are the curious ones

**Green Flag 3: Creator Offers Ideas**
- "Want me to do a '3-player game' breakdown?"
- Taking initiative
- Super rare, super valuable

## Measurement: How to Know If Outreach is Working

### Track Everything in a Spreadsheet

**Columns to Track:**

| Creator | Platform | Followers | Email | Date Sent | Response | Access? | Posts | Reach | Quality |
|---------|----------|-----------|-------|-----------|----------|---------|-------|-------|---------|
| @gazsgolf | IG | 87K | [email] | 4/1 | Yes | Yes | 2 | 150K | High |
| @hannahbleiner | TikTok | 70K | [email] | 4/1 | Yes | Yes | 1 | 80K | High |
| [name] | TikTok | 45K | [email] | 4/8 | No | No | 0 | — | — |

**Weekly Metrics to Pull:**

1. **Response Rate** = Responses ÷ Emails Sent (Target: 25–35%)
2. **Conversion Rate** = Access Taken ÷ Responses (Target: 60–70%)
3. **Post Rate** = Posts Published ÷ Access Given (Target: 50–70%)
4. **Avg Reach per Post** = Total Impressions ÷ Posts (Target: 20K–50K per creator)
5. **Engagement Rate** = Total Engagements ÷ Impressions (Target: 3–6%)
6. **Cost per Post** = Cash Spent ÷ Posts Published (Target: <$50/post in Phase 2)
7. **Cost per Acquisition** = Cash Spent ÷ New Users (Target: <$75 per user)

**Monthly Retrospective:**
- Which creators drove most traffic? (Name top 3)
- Which posts performed best? (Save these clips for reposts)
- Which content types worked? (Gameplay vs. education vs. funny)
- Response rate trending up or down? (Adjust pitch if down)
- Any unexpected wins? (Follow up with that creator)

## Special Case: Reaching Out to Big Creators

**These templates won't work for 500K+ followers.** Here's why and what to do instead:

**Why Cold Outreach Fails:**
- They get 100+ collab requests per week
- Your email gets lost
- They have agents/managers who screen everything

**What Works Instead:**
1. **Tier 1 (100K–500K followers):**
   - Still try cold email, but through manager if you can find contact
   - Offer higher fee ($500–$1,000)
   - Position as "sponsor a series episode" not "try the app"

2. **Tier 2 (500K–1M followers):**
   - Only reach out if you have revenue
   - Budget: $2K–5K per collab
   - Go through agent/manager (find on Influencer.co)
   - Position as: "Sponsor your next video series" or "Exclusive partnership"

3. **Tier 3 (1M+ followers like NLU, Good Good):**
   - Not feasible until you're bigger
   - Wait until Month 6–9 when you have traction
   - Approach through agent with specific offer
   - Budget: $3K–10K for single integration

**When Stick Has Proved Traction (Month 6+):**
- You'll have case studies: "50 micro-influencers, 500K+ impressions, 30 new users"
- That's the pitch to big creators
- "We've already validated this with your peers. Want to amplify?"

## Key Reminders

1. **Personalization wins.** Generic email = delete. Specific compliment = 3x better response rate.

2. **Keep it short.** 100–150 words is sweet spot. Respect their time.

3. **No scripts.** Don't ask them to use certain words. Let them be authentic.

4. **Follow up exactly once.** After 7 days, if no response, one gentle follow-up. Then move on.

5. **Real relationships > transactions.** The best creators are those who become friends. Check in even if they're not posting that week.

6. **Track everything.** You'll discover patterns. "Women golfers respond 40% better than men to education content." "TikTok creators post faster than YouTube creators." These insights inform how you scale.

7. **Pay fairly.** If someone drives 10 signups at $59.99/year and you're making $600, paying them $300–400 is cheap. They'll remember that.
