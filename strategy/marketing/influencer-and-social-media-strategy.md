# Stick Golf: Micro-Influencer & Social Media Marketing Strategy

**Date:** March 16, 2026
**Status:** Research-backed recommendation framework
**Audience:** Justin (founder) — bootstrapped growth strategy requiring $0 upfront spend, reinvestment model as revenue grows

---

## Executive Summary

Stick has a unique advantage in the golf app space: zero social media noise from competitors. Beezer, 18 Birdies, and Skins App have minimal content strategies. The golf betting game space has specific content creators (NLU, Good Good, Bob Does Sports, Tour Junkies) who actively create content around side bets and wagering.

**Core Strategic Insight:** Stick doesn't need a massive budget. The game is inherently shareable (people love showing off bets to friends), and the group organizer persona is hungry for tools. By focusing on micro-influencers ($100-500 per collab), grassroots seeding with free access, and leveraging existing golf betting creator communities, Stick can achieve meaningful traction before spending significant money.

**Phase 1 (Months 1-3, April-June 2026):** Outreach-only, zero spend. Free 1-year access to micro-influencers in exchange for gameplay footage.
**Phase 2 (Months 4-6, July-Sept 2026):** Modest spend ($5K-10K) on high-ROI creators + launch own content.
**Phase 3 (Months 7-12, Oct-2027):** Reinvestment model. If Phase 1-2 generate revenue, allocate 5-10% back to influencer deals and owned content.

## Part 1: Golf Micro-Influencer Landscape

### Follower-Based Pricing (2026 Market Rates)

**Micro-Influencer (10K–100K followers)**
- Instagram post: $100–$500
- TikTok video: $200–$800
- YouTube integration: $500+
- **Best value for bootstrapped brands:** TikTok and Instagram Reels (lower production cost, shorter format, easier to repurpose)

**Key Insight:** Over 80% of TikTok influencer collaborations in 2025 cost under $300. For Stick, this means most micro-influencer deals fall in the $150–$400 range per post.

**Why Micro-Influencers Win:**
- 4–10% engagement rate (vs. 1–2% for mega-influencers)
- Higher audience trust (feel like "a friend recommending something")
- 70% of Gen Z relate more to micro-influencers, with 57% higher purchase rate
- More willing to do flexible, long-term partnerships
- Better cost-per-engagement (CPE) and cost-per-acquisition (CPA)

## Part 3: Content Deal Structures & Deal Terms

### Recommended Deal Types for Stick Phase 1

**Deal Type 1: Free Product (Months 1–3, Bootstrap Phase)**

**Terms:**
- 1 year of free Stick Pro access (normally $59.99)
- Creator agrees to play 1–2 rounds on the app and post content
- Content rights: Stick can repost clips to its own channels (credited)
- No cash exchange

**When to use:** Tier A micro-influencers, creators with high brand alignment, creators building audience

**Expected outcome:** 3–8 organic video posts per creator, estimated reach 50K–200K per post (varies widely)

**Real math:** If Stick gets 10 micro-influencers to do this, that's 30–50 pieces of content, reaching 1.5–10M impressions organically (many with zero overlap). Cost: $600 ($59.99 × 10). ROI: Unknown but asymmetrical upside.

**Pros:**
- $0 cash outlay
- Creators are more authentic when not paid
- Easier to scale (message 50 creators, 10 convert)

**Cons:**
- No guaranteed deliverables
- Creator may never post or may post poorly
- Low control over narrative

---

**Deal Type 2: Flat Fee + Product (Months 4–6, Early Revenue Phase)**

**Terms:**
- Flat fee: $200–$400 (based on follower count and engagement rate)
- 1 year Stick Pro access (free)
- Guaranteed deliverables: 2 posts (TikTok + Instagram Reels), both posted within 2 weeks
- Content rights: Stick can repost

**When to use:** Tier A micro-influencers you want to guarantee content from, creators with proven engagement

**Expected outcome:** 2–4 reposts per creator, guaranteed reach tied to follower count + estimated engagement

**Real math:** If Stick spends $3K on 10 creators at $300 avg, cost per piece of content is $150. At 4% engagement rate and 50K average reach, that's roughly 8,000 engagements per piece, or $0.19 per engagement. For app awareness, this is reasonable.

**Pros:**
- Guaranteed content
- Predictable costs
- Easier to measure ROI

**Cons:**
- Requires cash outlay
- Creator is less authentic (feels transactional)

---

**Deal Type 3: Affiliate/Revenue Share (Months 6+, Growth Phase)**

**Terms:**
- Creator gets unique referral code (e.g., `GAZSGOLF`)
- Commission: 15–20% of revenue from users who sign up with that code (annual subscription value)
- Example: If creator drives 10 paid signups at $59.99/year, creator earns $90–$120/year per signup = $900–$1,200/year recurring

**When to use:** Long-term partnerships, creators with proven ability to drive conversions, when Stick has enough revenue to sustain commission payouts

**Expected outcome:** Recurring revenue streams, better long-term alignment

**Current reality:** Stick is pre-revenue. NOT recommended until Month 6+.

---

**Deal Type 4: Hybrid (Recommended for Tier A Partners)**

**Terms:**
- Base payment: $200 (covers content creation)
- Referral commission: 10% of revenue from signups using creator's code
- Product: 1 year Stick access

**When to use:** High-alignment creators you want ongoing relationships with

**Why this works:** Balances upfront motivation (the $200) with long-term skin-in-the-game (commission). Creator benefits from driving quality users, not just views.

## Part 5: TikTok & Instagram Strategy (Owned Content)

### Phase 1: Seeding & Micro-Content (Months 1–3)

**Goal:** Build social proof for outreach. Show creators examples of Stick in action.

**What to Post:**
1. **Gameplay clips** (15–30 seconds)
   - One round of Nassau at your course with commentary
   - Highlight the "what just happened?" moment (skins carryover, press decision, final tally)
   - Audio: Voiceover explaining the game + what Stick is automating
   - Post to TikTok + Instagram Reels (same clip, different captions)

2. **Education snippets** (15–30 seconds)
   - "What's a Nassau?" — quick explanation
   - "How does a press work?" — animation or text overlay
   - "Skins vs. Nassau" comparison
   - Post weekly

3. **Behind-the-scenes** (15–30 seconds)
   - Building screenshots, "here's what we shipped this week"
   - Your course, your testing rounds
   - Humanize the product

**Hashtag Strategy (Research-Backed):**

Core hashtags (low competition):
- #GolfBetting #GolfBettingGames #GolfSideGames #NassauGolf #SkinsGolf #WolfGolf

Trending hashtags:
- #GolfTips #GolfChallenge #GolfVlog #GolfCommunity #GolfIsLife #GolfVibes #GolfFun

Mix strategy: 3–6 hashtags per post, blend high-traffic (#GolfIsLife) with low-competition (#GolfBetting)

**Posting Cadence:** 3–4 posts per week to TikTok, repost key content to Instagram Reels. Build to 3K–5K followers by end of Month 3.

## Part 6: TikTok & Instagram Viral Content Principles

### What Makes Golf Content Go Viral?

**Research Findings:**
- High-energy challenges and head-to-head competitions perform best
- Personality-driven vlogging outperforms clinical instruction
- Funny/relatable moments (missed putts, bad decisions, banter) outperform straight tips
- Group dynamics (foursome reactions) get more engagement than single-player content

### Content Angles for Stick

**Angle 1: "The Press Moment"**
- Capture the exact moment someone decides to press (or not)
- Show the reaction, the stakes, the resolution
- Natural drama without forced narrative
- 15–30 seconds

**Angle 2: "Nobody Saw That Coming"**
- Skins carryover, sudden shift in scoreboard, unexpected winner
- Volatility is engagement bait (in the good way)
- "Watch this wolf trap everyone" type clips

**Angle 3: "The Bet Dispute"**
- Funny moment where people disagree on rules
- Resolution with Stick's automation
- "App settled it in 2 seconds" punch line
- Relatable to anyone who's ever had a threesome

**Angle 4: "Fast Settlement"**
- Record settling a 4-player round (all games)
- Show the whole settlement page with money amounts
- Audio: "Who won?" → *tap tap tap* → "Money moves"
- Emphasizes Stick's core value (instant settlement)

## Part 7: Grassroots Growth Tactics (Zero or Low Cost)

### Tactic 1: Golf Club Seeding

**What:** Offer Stick Pro free to 1–2 golf clubs in exchange for letting your players promote it.

**Why:** Golf clubs are communities. If the pro shop or booking app recommends it, adoption is easier.

**How:**
- Identify 5–10 public/semi-private clubs within 1 hour of your location
- Contact the head pro or course manager
- Pitch: "Free Stick access for your members for 3 months. Let's see if they like it."
- If they engage, ask if you can leave QR codes or flyers in the clubhouse

**Real outcome:** Small but engaged cohort, word-of-mouth within a tight community

**Effort:** Low. One email per club.

---

### Tactic 2: Golf Reddit & Forum Seeding

**Where:** r/golf, r/GolfBetting, GolfWRX forums, GolfChannel forums

**What:** Share Stick authentically when relevant (don't spam)

**Rules:**
- Only comment if the thread is about betting games or betting app recommendations
- Be honest: "I built this, but here's the honest assessment of what it does"
- Answer questions genuinely
- Don't self-promote unless asked

**Example thread:** "What's the best app for tracking Nassau bets?"
Your response: "We built Stick specifically for this. [Brief explanation]. It's free to try, $59.99/year after 3 rounds. Happy to answer questions."

**Real outcome:** Low-volume but high-intent traffic. Redditors are skeptical but convert well.

**Effort:** 30 minutes per week.

---

### Tactic 3: Content Partnerships (Non-Monetary)

**Concept:** Collaborate with creators on content neither of you could produce alone.

**Example 1: NLU/Good Good Parody**
- Film a "No Laying Up" style round with your buddies
- Use NLU's format: banter, swing breakdowns, betting games
- Post with credits to NLU (@ mention, link)
- Not a collab officially, but it's a love letter that might get their attention

**Example 2: Co-Hosted Settlement Guide**
- Reach out to a micro-influencer: "Want to make a '5-minute settlement guide' together?"
- You handle the app walkthrough, they handle the banter
- Post to both channels
- No money exchanged, mutual promotion

**Real outcome:** Content gets 2x reach (both audiences see it)

**Effort:** 2–3 hours per piece

---

### Tactic 4: Email Newsletter for Early Users

**What:** Build an email list from Day 1 of launch

**How:**
- Add email opt-in to homepage
- Early users who play 3 rounds get a "settlement cheat sheet" PDF for their email
- Weekly/biweekly emails: "Settling a Nassau," "How to press," "Rules you didn't know," "New game guide on the blog"
- Soft CTA: "Know other groups who'd love this? Refer them."

**Why:** Email has highest engagement rate of any channel. Every user in your list is a potential evangelist.

**Real outcome:** Baseline communication channel you own (not dependent on TikTok/Instagram algorithm)

**Effort:** 2–3 hours per email

## Part 8: Referral & Ambassador Program (Month 3+)

### Simple Referral Model

**What:**
- Every Stick user gets a unique referral link
- Friend signs up with that link → original user gets 1 month free for every 2 signups
- Example: User A refers 4 friends, gets 2 months free ($10 value)

**Why it works:** Turns every paid user into a salesperson. Aligns incentives.

**Implementation:**
- Add referral dashboard to app (show link, track clicks/signups)
- In-app prompt: "Know other groups? Share your link"
- Email campaigns: "Earn free months by referring friends"

**Cost:** Minimal (you're paying in $59.99/year increments, not cash)

## Part 12: Key Decisions & Recommendations

### Decision 1: TikTok vs Instagram vs YouTube

**Recommendation:** Start with TikTok + Instagram Reels (80% effort), YouTube long-form later (20% effort).

**Why:**
- Highest engagement rates for golf content
- Lowest production cost (just your phone + voiceover)
- Same clip repurposes across both platforms
- TikTok algorithm rewards engagement faster than YouTube

**YouTube strategy:** Start a channel, but upload content only when you have 3–4 minutes of good material. Better to post weekly with quality than daily with filler.

---

### Decision 2: Free Product vs Flat Fee for Influencers

**Recommendation:** Phase 1 = 100% free product. Phase 2 = 60% free / 40% flat fee. Phase 3 = Mix based on creator tier.

**Why:**
- Free product builds relationships and proves credibility
- Flat fee makes sense once you've proven the influencers drive real engagement
- Hybrid/affiliate makes sense once you have revenue

---

### Decision 3: Own Content vs Influencer Content

**Recommendation:** 50/50 initially, 70/30 (own) long-term.

**Why Phase 1 is 50/50:**
- You don't have enough follower base to produce daily
- Influencers provide social proof faster
- Influencer audience × 10 creators = bigger reach than Stick's small account

**Why Phase 3 becomes 70/30:**
- By Month 6–9, Stick will have 5K+ followers
- You'll have dozens of pieces of evergreen content
- Owned content compounds (ranks on YouTube, shared on Reddit, etc.)
- Influencers become sprints (monthly campaigns) rather than foundation

---

### Decision 4: Should Stick Have Instagram vs TikTok vs Both?

**Recommendation:** Both. One TikTok account, one Instagram account. Post the same clip to both (TikTok first, then Reels next day).

**Why:**
- TikTok algorithm is more aggressive for small accounts (easier to go viral)
- Instagram Reels has better visual discovery (search, explore page)
- Different audiences (TikTok = Gen Z, Instagram = older millennials)

**Setup:**
- @stickapp.golf (main brand) on both
- Post TikTok first (gain momentum)
- Screenshot top comment, add to Reels caption (social proof)
- Stagger by 12–24 hours to hit each platform's algorithm independently

---

### Decision 5: How to Handle Betting/Gambling Sensitivity

**Reality:** Stick is a betting app. Some platforms and creators are sensitive to gambling content.

**Approach:**
- Position as "game tracking" not "gambling app"
- Emphasize "your friends, your foursomes, your club"
- Use language: "settle bets" not "place bets"
- Highlight legitimacy: "All 50 states, verified math, zero house edge"

**Specific considerations:**
- Don't reach out to creators under 18 or obviously under-21 audiences
- Avoid creators with prior gambling controversy
- Be transparent in outreach: "Stick is a golf betting app" — don't hide it

## Part 14: What NOT to Do

1. **Don't Spam Subreddits.** One authentic comment per thread. Reddit bans spammers quickly.
2. **Don't Expect Mega-Influencers to Reply.** NLU, Good Good, Bob Does Sports won't notice until you're big. Wait until Month 6+.
3. **Don't Overproduce Content.** 3 posts/week beats 1 video per day. Quality > quantity.
4. **Don't Oversell in Influencer Outreach.** Let the app sell itself. You're just providing access.
5. **Don't Post Without a Clear Value Prop.** Every post should answer: "Why would I share this?"
6. **Don't Ignore Engagement.** If someone comments, reply. Community beats algorithms.
7. **Don't Chase Viral Moments.** Consistency beats virality. Post regularly, let the algorithm work.
