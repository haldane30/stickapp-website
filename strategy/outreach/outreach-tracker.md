## How This Works

Every target moves through:

```
Identified → Research Done → Contacted → Replied → In Negotiation → Closed (Won/Lost/Parked)
```

**Identified** = we know they exist and seem like a fit
**Research Done** = confirmed audience, found contact, understood their content style
**Contacted** = outreach sent, waiting
**Replied** = they responded (positive or asking questions)
**In Negotiation** = discussing terms
**Closed Won** = deal made
**Closed Lost** = hard no or no response after 2 follow-ups
**Parked** = good fit but wrong timing (too early, wrong season)

#### Research Notes (March 12, 2026)

**What we learned:** The micro-influencer space for golf betting content is thin. Most golf YouTubers in the 5K–50K range are course vloggers, swing coaches, or equipment reviewers — not money-game focused. The creators who specifically film Nassau/Skins/Wolf rounds tend to be either very small (<1K) or very large (100K+). This means:

1. **Broaden the definition.** Don't just target "golf betting" creators. Target golf vloggers who play competitive rounds with friends and would naturally integrate Stick into their content. Any channel that films a foursome playing a round is a potential partner — they just haven't been tracking bets on camera because it was too hard. Stick changes that.

2. **Instagram and TikTok are where the action is.** Golf betting content lives in short-form clips (settlement arguments, "you owe me" moments, scorecard reveals). Search these hashtags manually: `#golfbetting` `#nassaugolf` `#golfmoneygame` `#golfgambling` `#saturdaygolf` `#golfwithfriends` `#wolfgolfgame`

3. **The podcast tier overlaps.** Chasing Scratch, The Fried Egg, and EAL all have YouTube channels too. The podcast outreach could include a "film a round with Stick" angle.

## How to Find Micro-Influencer Targets

### Search Terms to Use on YouTube
- "golf money game" / "golf betting game"
- "nassau golf round" / "wolf golf game round"
- "golf side bet" / "golf gambling"
- "playing for money golf" / "golf skins game"
- "real money golf" / "golf betting with friends"

### What to Look For
- **Content style:** They show actual money rounds on camera, not just tips/instruction
- **Audience:** Comments feel like golfers who actually play — not aspirational beginners
- **Engagement rate:** More important than follower count. 5% engagement on 20K > 0.5% on 200K
- **Posting frequency:** Active in the last 90 days
- **Fit signal:** They've complained about settlement math, score keeping, or "nobody knowing the rules"

### Instagram / TikTok Search Terms
- `#golfbetting` `#nassaugolf` `#wolfgame` `#golfmoneygame`
- `#saturdaygolf` `#golfgambling` `#skingame`

### Reddit
- r/golf — find threads about money games, identify top commenters with valuable answers
- r/discgolf — not our space, but similar culture
- Golf betting threads — the people asking/answering detailed rule questions are power users

## Outreach Rules

1. **Always research before contacting.** Watch at least 2 videos or read 3 posts. Reference something specific in your outreach.
2. **Lead with the product, not money.** Golf creators are golfers first.
3. **One follow-up only.** If no response after 7 days, send one follow-up. After that, park it.
4. **Don't pitch anyone under 1K followers.** Too small to move the needle even for free seedings.
5. **Don't pitch anyone over 500K cold.** They have agents. You need a warm intro at that level.
6. **Update this doc immediately after sending.** Stale trackers are useless.
