## The Strategy (60-Second Version)

**Who to pitch:**
1. **Podcasts first.** No Laying Up, The Fried Egg. Higher chance of yes, lower barrier to entry.
2. **Then publications.** GOLF Magazine, Golf Digest. Bigger reach, longer lead time.
3. **Then influencers.** Golf reporters on Twitter, bloggers, YouTubers.

**When to pitch:**
- **April 2-5 (1 week before launch):** Podcasts and digital outlets
- **April 6 (2 days before):** News briefs to GOLF.com, GolfWeek
- **April 7-13 (Masters week):** Avoid heavy pitching; focus on pre-scheduled appearances
- **April 14+ (after):** Features to monthly magazines (they'll have breathing room)

**How to pitch:**
- Listen/read 3 recent episodes or articles first
- Keep pitch short (100-200 words max)
- Lead with the problem ("settlement disputes"), not the product
- Personalize with specific references
- Follow up once after 3-5 days if no response
- Then move on

**Should you hire a PR firm?**
- **No, not yet.** You have 4-6 weeks until launch. DIY is cost-effective and you'll get better results because you know the product.
- **You are well-positioned:** Founder story is strong, product is authentic, golf media values that.
- **If you hire post-launch** (and it makes sense): Boutique golf PR firm runs $12K-$20K/month.

**What success looks like (first 30 days):**
- 2-3 podcast guest appearances (1-2 from Tier 1 shows)
- 1-2 publication features (GOLF.com, GolfWeek, or Golf Digest)
- 50-200+ golf media social mentions
- Awareness and credibility boost (metrics matter less than reaching the right audience)

## Key Findings from Research

### Golf Media Landscape

- **Golf Digest:** Large circulation (1.66M), selective app coverage, editorial gate. Pitch through editors@golfdigest.com.
- **GOLF Magazine:** More tech-forward, larger digital reach (6.53M). Better odds than Digest. Pitch Brendan Mohler (Brendan_Mohler@Golf.com).
- **GolfWeek:** PGA Tour-focused. Less app coverage. Harder pitch.
- **The Fried Egg:** Golf course design podcast. Founder-friendly. Host (Geoff Shackelford) is accessible.

### Golf Podcasts (Best ROI)

- **No Laying Up:** 200K+ downloads/ep. Founder stories are their jam. Host(s): Ryan, Charlie, etc. PITCH THIS.
- **The Fried Egg:** 50K-100K monthly. Geoff Shackelford (founder) is opinionated and open. PITCH THIS.
- **Fore Play (Barstool):** 300K+ downloads/ep. More entertainment-focused. Medium difficulty.
- **The Clubhouse:** PGA Tour focus. Medium difficulty.

### Costs (If You Want to Pay)

- **Golf Digest full-page print ad:** ~$88K (not viable pre-launch)
- **Sponsored content:** $5K-$15K per piece
- **Podcast sponsorship:** $1.5K-$6K per episode (worth it post-launch if you have data)
- **PR firm retainer:** $12K-$20K/month (wait until post-launch if at all)

### Masters Week Timing

- **Don't pitch during tournament week (April 7-13).** Media will be saturated.
- **Do pitch April 2-5.** Golf media is focused on golf, but pre-tournament chaos.
- **Optimal: April 6 (day before)** for quick-turnaround digital outlets.
- **April 14+ (after).** Best for longer features. Editors will have breathing room.

### DIY PR Reality

- **Time commitment:** 5-10 hours/week for 4-6 weeks around launch. Then 2-3 hours/week maintenance.
- **Cost:** ~$0 if you do it yourself. ~$12K-$20K/month if you hire a firm.
- **Your advantage:** You can pitch authentically (you built the product), you know the nuances, you can respond quickly to inbound interest.
- **87% of journalists prefer email pitches** (not calls, not meetings first).

## Key Assumptions & Caveats

1. **This research is current as of March 16, 2026.** Contact info and publication policies can change. Always verify email addresses on official websites before pitching.

2. **Golf media is small and interconnected.** Be respectful, authentic, and follow up thoughtfully. Bad pitches or ghosting will be remembered.

3. **Masters Week timing is tight.** Plan for 2-3 week lead times on magazine features, 1-2 weeks on podcasts, 1-2 days on digital news.

4. **Paid media (ads, sponsorships) is expensive.** Only consider if you have post-launch data showing ROI.

5. **Podcasts are your highest-ROI channel pre-launch.** They have lower barriers to entry, more accessible hosts, and highly engaged audiences.

6. **Your founder story is strong.** Use it. Authenticity beats polish in golf media.
