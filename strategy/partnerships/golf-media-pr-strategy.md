# Golf Media & PR Strategy for Stick Launch

**Last Updated:** March 16, 2026
**Context:** Stick launches around Masters Week (April 7-13, 2026). This doc provides actionable guidance for getting Stick featured in golf media and podcasts as a bootstrapped founder.

---

## Executive Summary

The golf media landscape is fragmented but generally receptive to niche products like Stick. Key findings:

1. **Earned media (reviews) is available but requires strong pitches** — Golf publications do cover apps, but you'll compete against equipment manufacturers for attention
2. **Paid sponsorships are expensive** ($2K-$20K+/month for major outlets) and likely not cost-effective pre-launch
3. **Podcasts are the highest-ROI channel** — Lower barrier to entry, highly engaged audiences, and podcast hosts are more accessible than magazine editors
4. **Masters Week is a double-edged sword** — Media will be saturated, but also most focused on golf content. A pre-Masters pitch has better odds than during the tournament itself
5. **DIY PR makes sense for a bootstrapped founder** — You have a compelling founder story, product authenticity, and specific differentiation that resonate with golf audiences. Hiring a PR firm ($12K-$20K/month) doesn't make ROI sense pre-launch.

## Part 2: Paid vs. Earned Media in Golf

### Paid Placements (Sponsored Content)

**What it costs:**

| Medium | Cost Range | Details |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Golf Digest full-page print ad** | ~$88,000 one-time | Very expensive. 1.66M circulation. Full production costs on top. |
| **GOLF Magazine print ad** | $40K-$80K one-time | Comparable to Digest. Slightly cheaper, larger digital reach. |
| **Golf publication sponsored content (digital)** | $5K-$15K per piece | Cheaper than ads. You provide content, they feature it. More integration into editorial environment. |
| **Golf.com/GolfWeek digital sponsorship** | $10K-$25K/month retainer | Ongoing placements, social amplification, newsletter mentions. |
| **Podcast sponsorship (major shows)** | $2K-$8K per episode | (See Part 3 below) |

**Reality Check:** At $59.99/year revenue, a single $88K print ad in Golf Digest requires 1,467 annual subscribers to break even. This is not viable pre-launch. Sponsored content at $10K is more reasonable, but only after you've validated that the target audience converts.

**When paid makes sense:** Post-launch, once you have downloads/trial conversion data. Sponsorships in golf podcasts (below) are the exception—they offer better ROI and lower cost.

### Earned Media (Reviews, Features)

**How it works:**

1. You pitch a story idea to an editor or journalist
2. They assign it (or don't)
3. They write it (or assign a freelancer)
4. It publishes without you paying for placement
5. You get credibility + reach

**Pros:**
- Free. Costs you time, not money.
- Credible. Readers trust editorial more than ads.
- Compound. A single feature in Golf Digest sends traffic for months.

**Cons:**
- Competitive. Editors get pitched constantly.
- Slow. Lead time is 2-4 weeks (monthly print) or 1-2 weeks (digital).
- No guarantees. Pitch ≠ coverage.

**Your advantage:** You have a strong founder story (25 years design experience, solo founder, pre-launch exclusivity) and a real innovation (the only app with press-the-press chains, settlement verification, etc.). Editors in the golf space value authenticity.

**Key Finding:** No Laying Up and The Fried Egg are your best bets. Both are founder-friendly, reach engaged golfers (not casual), and hosts respect authentic products built by real people.

### What a Podcast Pitch Looks Like

**Do's:**
- Listen to at least 2-3 recent episodes before pitching
- Keep pitch to 100-150 words max
- Lead with the insight, not the product ("We built an app because settlement disputes kill friendships" → "We noticed golfers spend 5% of round playing, 95% settling bets")
- Mention why it fits *their* show specifically
- Include 2-3 conversation angles (multi-game parity, the closest-to-pin problem, Quota's scaling for club events)

**Don'ts:**
- Generic pitch ("Hey, we have a golf app!")
- Pitching to a show you haven't listened to (they'll know)
- Making it about the product launch ("You can help promote our App Store launch!")
- Being passive ("If you're interested let me know")
- Asking for a favor ("Would you consider featuring Stick?")

**Example Pitch (100 words):**

> "Hi [Host],
>
> I've been listening to your show for a few weeks and loved your [specific episode]. You hit on the exact problem we built Stick to solve: golfers spend an hour arguing about carryover rules instead of enjoying the round.
>
> I'm Justin, a designer who spent 25 years at ad agencies before building this. Stick is a betting app with 12 games (Nassau, Skins, Wolf, etc.) where the game engine settles disputes automatically. We've seen the same bug in every competitor: proxies are 50% disputed.
>
> Thought your audience would find the product angle interesting. Launching late March for Masters Week. Happy to chat. —Justin"

## Part 4: Masters Week Timing (April 7-13, 2026)

### The Good News

- **Golf media is hyper-focused on golf.** If you pitch a day or two before Masters week, editors have golf on the brain. This is actually good timing for a golf app pitch.
- **Your launch aligns with the sport's biggest moment.** You can authentically tie your launch to Masters week ("The week golf gets interesting, we launch Stick").

### The Bad News

- **Media will be overwhelmed.** Golf reporters will be covering player news, course conditions, betting odds, fantasy picks. Your pitch is competing against Rory's form, Brooks' new swing, Augusta course updates.
- **Editor attention is lowest.** Many reporters will be on-site in Augusta during the tournament itself (April 9-13). The days before (April 6-8) are peak chaos internally.
- **Embargo risk.** Some publications prefer not to publish major features during tournament week.

### Optimal Timing Strategy

**Do not pitch during Masters week itself (April 7-13).**

Instead:

1. **April 2-5 (1 week before):** Pitch to podcasts and digital-first outlets. "We're launching for Masters Week" is a good hook. Podcast hosts can record 1-2 weeks ahead.
2. **April 6 (2 days before):** Pitch a short feature/news piece to GOLF.com and GolfWeek digital teams. They update multiple times daily and may slot you in as a news brief.
3. **April 7-13 (Masters week):** Focus on podcasts that are live during the week (daily pods). Podcast guest spots are easier to schedule mid-week as counter-programming to tournament coverage.
4. **April 14+ (Week after):** Pitch longer features to monthly magazines (Digest, GolfWeek print). Editors will have breathing room and may be more receptive to "Here's the app everyone's talking about from Masters week."

**Better framing:** "Launching for Masters Week" is honest timing. But frame the pitch around the *problem*, not the event. Example:

> "Every golfer watching Masters week will be thinking about betting pools, side games, and settlement rules. We built Stick to solve the #1 problem: disputes. Here's our story."

## Part 5: PR Strategy for Bootstrapped Founders (DIY vs. Agency)

### Should You Hire a PR Firm?

**Cost of a boutique golf PR firm:**
- Entry-level retainer: $5K-$8K/month
- Standard boutique: $12K-$20K/month
- Mid-size agency: $25K+/month

**Your situation:** Pre-launch, bootstrapped, no investor/VC backing. Hiring a PR firm costs $60K-$240K/year. Unless you have a clear ROI path (e.g., you're raising VC and need media buzz for investor meetings), this doesn't make sense.

### DIY PR Framework (Recommended)

You are well-positioned to do PR yourself. Here's why:

1. **You have a compelling founder story.** 25 years of design experience → solo founder making a bet on a passion problem. This is exactly what golf media loves.
2. **You have authentic differentiation.** Your app isn't "another betting app." It has specific, defensible features (press chains, settlement verification, 12-game depth).
3. **Golf is a relationship-driven industry.** Editors know the community. If you engage authentically, relationships compound.
4. **Your time has value.** Spending 5 hours/week on PR is cheaper than paying an agency, and you'll get better results because you know the product intimately.

**Time commitment:** 5-10 hours/week for 4-6 weeks around launch. After that, drops to 2-3 hours/week maintenance (responding to inbound press, pitching one piece every 1-2 weeks).

### The DIY PR Playbook

**Step 1: Build your media list (2-3 hours)**

Create a simple spreadsheet with:
- Publication name
- Contact name (editor, golf reporter, podcast host)
- Email address
- Recent piece they published (as research)
- Pitch angle (how Stick fits their coverage area)

**Tier 1 Contacts (high priority):**
- Golf Digest (editors@golfdigest.com)
- GOLF Magazine (Brendan_Mohler@Golf.com)
- No Laying Up Podcast (podcast hosts)
- The Fried Egg (Geoff Shackelford)

**Tier 2 Contacts (secondary):**
- GolfWeek editorial
- Golf.com digital team
- Fore Play / Barstool
- Individual golf journalists on Twitter

**Tier 3 Contacts (ongoing):**
- Golf bloggers, YouTubers, Twitter influencers
- Local media (if launching in your region first)

**Step 2: Craft your core pitch (2-3 hours)**

You need **three pitch versions:**

**Version A: Podcast guest pitch (100 words)**
- For: No Laying Up, The Fried Egg, similar shows
- Angle: "Designer built betting app to solve real settlement problems"
- Tone: Casual, conversational, forward-looking

**Version B: Publication feature pitch (150-200 words)**
- For: GOLF Magazine, GolfWeek, Golf Digest
- Angle: "How one designer is fixing the $50B golf betting market"
- Tone: Professional but personable

**Version C: News brief pitch (50-75 words)**
- For: GOLF.com, GolfWeek digital, breaking news
- Angle: "Stick launches for Masters Week; here's what's different"
- Tone: Newsy, current

**Key elements in all three:**
- Your credibility (25 years design)
- The problem (settlement disputes, limited game options)
- Your insight (why existing apps miss the mark)
- The proof (your specific features vs. competitors)
- The timing (Masters week launch)

**Step 3: Outreach (ongoing, 3-5 hours/week)**

Week 1 (March 20-26):
- Email Tier 1 podcast hosts + GOLF Magazine
- Personalize each pitch (reference a recent episode/article)
- Follow up after 3 days if no response

Week 2 (March 27-April 2):
- Email Golf Digest + GolfWeek if no response from Week 1
- Podcast pitches should start landing here (record early)
- Twitter pitches (send DMs to golf reporters/influencers)

Week 3+ (April 3+):
- Follow up on outstanding pitches
- Respond to inbound press inquiries quickly
- Prepare talking points for any scheduled interviews

**Step 4: Prepare for interviews (3-4 hours)**

Once you land a podcast or interview, prepare:
- 3-4 key talking points (keep to 30 seconds each)
- 2-3 real settlement examples (have them ready to tell as stories)
- A story about why you built this (the origin moment)
- Competitive context (be honest about what others are doing, what you're not)
- Call-to-action for the audience ("Sign up, play 3 free rounds")

**Step 5: Track and iterate (1 hour/week)**

Keep a simple tracker:
- Pitch sent date
- Follow-up sent date
- Response received? (Yes/No)
- If yes: scheduled date, media contact info, talking points sent
- If no: reason (too busy, bad timing, not a fit)

After 2-3 weeks of outreach, you'll see patterns. Which publication/podcast type responds best? Refine your pitches based on what lands.

### When to Reconsider a PR Firm

If, after 4-6 weeks of DIY outreach:
- You've landed 2-3 major features + 4-5 podcast interviews, *and*
- You're seeing strong user acquisition from that coverage, *and*
- You have revenue/funding to support ongoing paid PR,

...then hiring a boutique PR firm for $8K-$12K/month might make sense for scaling coverage.

But pre-launch, DIY is the move.

## Part 6: Press Release Strategy

### Do You Need a Press Release?

**Yes, but with caveats:**

A press release is **not** the same as a pitch. A press release is a one-size-fits-all announcement. A pitch is personalized.

You should issue a press release **only after** you've landed at least one major feature or podcast interview. Here's why:

1. **Timing.** A press release published before any coverage lands looks like you're trying to create news out of nothing. It tanks credibility.
2. **Newsworthiness.** Editors see hundreds of press releases. Yours will be ignored unless it arrives *as news*, not *as a press blast*.
3. **Sequences better.** A podcast interview goes live → that's news → press release amplifies the news.

### Press Release Template

When you're ready to issue one, use this structure:

```
STICK GOLF BETTING APP LAUNCHES FOR MASTERS WEEK WITH 12 GAMES, REAL-TIME SETTLEMENT

Designer-Built App Solves Golf's #1 Problem: Bet Disputes

[CITY, STATE] – March [DATE], 2026 – Stick, a premium golf betting app, launches today
for Masters Week with 12 authentic golf games (Nassau, Skins, Wolf, Snake, Match Play,
Nine Point, Split Sixes, Sixes, Junk, Vegas, Scotch, Quota) and automated settlement
that eliminates disputes.

Built by Justin [LAST NAME], a veteran UI/UX designer with 25 years of creative direction
at top marketing agencies, Stick addresses a problem every golfer faces: how to handle
tie-breaking, carryovers, and settlement without arguments in the parking lot.

"Every foursome plays by different rules," Justin said. "One person's Nassau has a hammer
rule, another's doesn't. Someone's Skins carry over, someone's don't. Stick handles all
of it—you pick your house rules once, the app settles the bets automatically. No disputes."

The app features:
- 12 golf games with full rules customization
- Real-time settlement with zero-sum verification
- Multi-device sync (all players see the same game state)
- Proximity tracking with manual entry (solves the 'who hit it closest' dispute)
- Support for 2-4 players, with architecture built for club events (30+ players)

Pricing: Free to join rounds. Three rounds free to create. $59.99/year or $7.99/month to
create unlimited rounds.

Stick launches on the App Store and Google Play [DATE].

Download Stick: [APP STORE LINK]

###

**About Stick**
[1-2 sentences about the company]

**Contact**
Justin [LAST NAME]
Stick Golf
[EMAIL]
[PHONE]
```

### Where to Distribute

- Press release wire services (PR.com, PRWeb, etc.) — but wait until after first coverage lands
- Direct to your media list
- Tweet from your account
- Email to your waiting list

**Cost:** Most wire services cost $200-$600. DIY is fine initially (just email to contacts).

## Part 10: Realistic Expectations

### What Success Looks Like (First 3 Months)

**Tier 1 (Excellent):**
- 1-2 podcast guest appearances (No Laying Up, The Fried Egg, or similar)
- 1 feature in GOLF.com or GolfWeek digital
- 50+ golf media social mentions

**Tier 2 (Good):**
- 2-3 podcast guest appearances
- 2-3 golf blog/YouTube features
- 200+ golf media social mentions
- "Trending" in golf Twitter for a day or two

**Tier 3 (Solid):**
- 4+ podcast appearances
- 1 Golf Digest or GOLF Magazine feature
- 500+ social mentions
- Organic feature requests from golfers on Reddit/forums

### What To Avoid

- Don't pitch the same person twice without new information. You'll burn that contact.
- Don't claim you're "disrupting golf betting" without proof. Claims need backing.
- Don't bad-mouth competitors. Golf is a small world; generosity beats snark.
- Don't send generic pitches. "Hello," + personalization = 10x response rate.
- Don't expect immediate results. PR is a 6-12 month play. Benefits compound.
