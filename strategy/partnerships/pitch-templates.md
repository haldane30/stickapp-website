## Quick Personalization Checklist

Before sending any pitch:

- [ ] Research the outlet/host. Mention a specific recent episode/article.
- [ ] Check the tone. Podcast pitches are casual; publication pitches are professional.
- [ ] Keep it short. Podcast (100 words), publication (150-200 words), news (50-75 words).
- [ ] Lead with the problem, not the product.
- [ ] Include one specific talking point (e.g., "proximity is disputed 40% of the time").
- [ ] Personalize the subject line (reference their work, not just "pitch").
- [ ] Include your email and phone number.
- [ ] Don't make false claims. Check against your feature audit.
- [ ] Proofread. One typo = delete button.

## Podcast Recording Logistics (If You Land an Appearance)

**Before the recording:**

1. Send talking points (3-4 key themes, with 30-second max explanations each)
2. Ask if they want real settlement examples (have 2-3 short ones ready)
3. Confirm: audio quality, recording time, release date, if you can share it
4. Test audio setup: quiet room, good mic, internet stable

**During recording:**

1. Speak naturally. Avoid corporate jargon ("disruptive innovation," etc.).
2. Tell stories, not features. Instead of "12 games," say "my buddy asked me to add Quota because he runs a club event with 20 players."
3. Be honest about gaps. "We don't do [thing], but here's what we do instead."
4. Give specific numbers when you have them (proximity disputes down 40%, etc.)
5. Call to action: "Download Stick, play 3 free rounds, see what you think."

**After recording:**

1. Send thank-you email with app link + promo code (if applicable)
2. Ask when it publishes + request link
3. Share it everywhere (your email, Twitter, Discord)
4. Track UTM parameters to see if podcast listeners convert

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Generic pitch ("Hey, we have a golf app!")**
✓ **Specific pitch ("I built an app that stops proximity disputes by letting players manually enter who hit it closest")**

❌ **Pitching a show without listening to recent episodes**
✓ **Listening to 3-4 recent episodes and referencing a specific moment**

❌ **Claiming you're "disrupting golf betting"**
✓ **Saying "we handle settlement verification better than existing apps because..."**

❌ **Pitching multiple times after rejection**
✓ **One follow-up after 3-5 days, then move on**

❌ **Long pitches (300+ words)**
✓ **Short pitches (100-200 words) that hook fast**

❌ **Making false product claims**
✓ **Verifying against your feature audit before pitching**

❌ **Bad-mouthing competitors**
✓ **Being respectful: "Other apps handle this differently, but we chose to..."**

## Success Story Format (Use After Launch)

Once you have metrics (downloads, retention, user testimonials), use this template
for follow-up pitches or guest spots:

```
"After launching [DATE], Stick hit [METRIC] in [TIMEFRAME]. Users have consistently
told us [INSIGHT]. This is what they're doing with the app: [REAL USE CASE]."

Example: "After launching March 15, we hit 5,000 downloads in the first week. Users
told us the biggest impact was not having to argue about proximity bets anymore.
One group reported going from a parking lot argument every third round to zero

```

This is gold for podcast hosts and journalists. **Proof beats promises.**
